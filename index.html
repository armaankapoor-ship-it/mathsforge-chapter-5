01Continuity

Continuity at a point

f is continuous at x=a if left limit, right limit, and f(a) are equal.

  • CBSE writing: Write all three conditions clearly.
  • JEE focus: Piecewise functions often test parameter values.
  • Common trap: Limit existing is not enough if f(a) differs.
02Continuity

Continuity on interval

A function is continuous on an interval if it is continuous at every point of the interval.

  • CBSE writing: Check endpoints with one-sided limits.
  • JEE focus: Endpoint treatment matters in closed intervals.
  • Common trap: Do not use two-sided limit at an endpoint.
03Differentiability

Differentiability at a point

Differentiability needs equal left-hand and right-hand derivatives.

  • CBSE writing: For modulus and piecewise functions, compare LHD and RHD.
  • JEE focus: Corners and cusps are not differentiable.
  • Common trap: Continuity does not always imply differentiability.
04Connection

Differentiability implies continuity

If a function is differentiable at a point, then it is continuous there.

  • CBSE writing: State this implication in proof questions.
  • JEE focus: Use contrapositive: discontinuity means not differentiable.
  • Common trap: The converse is false.
05Rules

Product and quotient rules

Product and quotient derivatives must be applied in correct order.

  • CBSE writing: Show u and v before using formulas.
  • JEE focus: Avoid expanding when product rule is faster.
  • Common trap: Quotient rule numerator order is important.
06Chain

Chain rule

For composite functions, differentiate outside first, then multiply by derivative of inside.

  • CBSE writing: Use substitution u=g(x) for clarity.
  • JEE focus: Nested functions are common in objective problems.
  • Common trap: Do not forget inner derivative.
07Implicit

Implicit differentiation

When differentiating y terms with respect to x, multiply by dy/dx.

  • CBSE writing: Collect dy/dx terms and solve.
  • JEE focus: Useful for curves and parametric-looking equations.
  • Common trap: Derivative of y^2 is 2y dy/dx, not 2y.
08Log

Logarithmic differentiation

Take log on both sides to simplify variable powers, products, and quotients.

  • CBSE writing: Use log rules carefully before differentiating.
  • JEE focus: Power-variable expressions become easy.
  • Common trap: Use absolute values when needed in logs.

Formula sheet

High-yield formulas with exact usage conditions.

Use these as quick revision cards before solving examples.

Continuity

Continuity condition

lim x->a- f(x) = lim x->a+ f(x) = f(a)

Limit and value meet.

  • Example: Use for piecewise parameter questions.
  • Trap: Both one-sided limits are needed.
Derivative

First principle

f'(a)=lim h->0 [f(a+h)-f(a)]/h

Definition of derivative at a point.

  • Example: Use to prove derivative of x^2 is 2x.
  • Trap: Limit must exist and be finite.
Rules

Product rule

(uv) prime = u prime v + u v prime

Differentiate one factor at a time.

  • Example: d(x sin x)=sin x + x cos x.
  • Trap: Do not multiply derivatives only.
Rules

Quotient rule

(u/v) prime = (v u prime - u v prime)/v^2

Bottom times top derivative minus top times bottom derivative.

  • Example: d(x/(x+1)) = 1/(x+1)^2.
  • Trap: Numerator order matters.
Chain

Chain rule

d/dx f(g(x)) = f prime(g(x)) g prime(x)

Outside derivative times inside derivative.

  • Example: d(sin x^2)=2x cos x^2.
  • Trap: Do not miss 2x.
Trig

Basic trig derivatives

d(sin x)=cos x, d(cos x)=-sin x, d(tan x)=sec^2 x

Core trigonometric derivatives.

  • Example: d(tan 3x)=3sec^2 3x.
  • Trap: Use radians.
Inverse

Inverse trig derivatives

d(sin^-1 x)=1/sqrt(1-x^2), d(tan^-1 x)=1/(1+x^2)

Important for Chapter 5 formulas.

  • Example: d(tan^-1 2x)=2/(1+4x^2).
  • Trap: Chain rule still applies.
Log

Log derivative

d(log x)=1/x, d(e^x)=e^x

Exponential and logarithmic core rules.

  • Example: d(log(3x+1))=3/(3x+1).
  • Trap: Domain of log matters.

Derivation bank

Proofs that can be reproduced in exams.

Open a proof, follow the steps, and check the final result.

Prove differentiability implies continuity
  1. Assume f is differentiable at a.
  2. Write f(x)-f(a) = [(f(x)-f(a))/(x-a)](x-a).
  3. As x approaches a, first factor approaches f prime(a), second approaches 0.
  4. Thus f(x)-f(a) approaches 0, so f(x) approaches f(a).

Final: Differentiable => continuous.

Shortcut: Write f(x)-f(a) as product.

Warning: The converse is false.

Derivative of x^2 by first principle
  1. f(x+h)-f(x)=(x+h)^2-x^2.
  2. This equals 2xh+h^2.
  3. Divide by h to get 2x+h.
  4. Let h approach 0 to get 2x.

Final: f'(x)=2x

Shortcut: Expand (x+h)^2.

Warning: Cancel h before taking limit.

Product rule proof idea
  1. Write difference quotient for u(x)v(x).
  2. Add and subtract u(x+h)v(x).
  3. Split into two limits.
  4. Use derivative definitions and continuity to get the formula.

Final: (uv) prime = u prime v + u v prime.

Shortcut: Add and subtract u(x+h)v(x).

Warning: Continuity of differentiable functions is used.

Implicit differentiation method
  1. Differentiate both sides with respect to x.
  2. For y terms, multiply by dy/dx.
  3. Move dy/dx terms to one side.
  4. Factor and solve for dy/dx.

Final: Collect dy/dx terms.

Shortcut: Treat y as function of x.

Warning: Every y derivative carries dy/dx.

Practice bank

Exam-style questions with answer reveal.

Filter by keyword, try the question first, then reveal the answer and trap.

CBSEEasy

1. State condition for continuity at x=a.

Answer: LHL=RHL=f(a)

Both one-sided limits and function value must be equal.

Trap: Limit alone is not enough.

DerivativeEasy

2. Differentiate x^5.

Answer: 5x^4

Use power rule d(x^n)=nx^(n-1).

Trap: Subtract one from exponent.

ProductMedium

3. Differentiate x sin x.

Answer: sin x + x cos x

Use product rule.

Trap: Do not write cos x only.

ChainMedium

4. Differentiate sin(x^2).

Answer: 2x cos(x^2)

Outside derivative cos(x^2), inside derivative 2x.

Trap: Do not forget 2x.

QuotientMedium

5. Differentiate x/(x+1).

Answer: 1/(x+1)^2

[(x+1)*1-x*1]/(x+1)^2.

Trap: Numerator order matters.

ImplicitMedium

6. Differentiate x^2+y^2=25.

Answer: dy/dx=-x/y

2x+2y dy/dx=0.

Trap: Derivative of y^2 includes dy/dx.

ContinuityHard

7. If f is differentiable at a, is it continuous at a?

Answer: Yes

Differentiability implies continuity.

Trap: Continuity does not imply differentiability.

TrapMedium

8. Is |x| differentiable at x=0?

Answer: No

LHD=-1 and RHD=1.

Trap: It is continuous but not differentiable.

LogMedium

9. Differentiate log(3x+1).

Answer: 3/(3x+1)

Use chain rule with log u.

Trap: Do not write 1/(3x+1) only.

InverseHard

10. Differentiate tan^-1(2x).

Answer: 2/(1+4x^2)

Derivative is u prime/(1+u^2) with u=2x.

Trap: Chain rule applies.

SecondMedium

11. If y=x^3, find d2y/dx2.

Answer: 6x

First derivative 3x^2, second derivative 6x.

Trap: Do not stop at first derivative.

CBSEHard

12. Find derivative of y=x^x.

Answer: x^x(1+log x)

Take log: log y=x log x, then y prime/y=log x+1.

Trap: Power rule alone does not apply.

Core formulas

  • Continuity condition: lim x->a- f(x) = lim x->a+ f(x) = f(a)
  • First principle: f'(a)=lim h->0 [f(a+h)-f(a)]/h
  • Product rule: (uv) prime = u prime v + u v prime
  • Quotient rule: (u/v) prime = (v u prime - u v prime)/v^2
  • Chain rule: d/dx f(g(x)) = f prime(g(x)) g prime(x)
  • Basic trig derivatives: d(sin x)=cos x, d(cos x)=-sin x, d(tan x)=sec^2 x

Common traps

  • Limit must equal function value for continuity.
  • A continuous function may not be differentiable.
  • Chain rule inner derivative is compulsory.
  • Quotient rule numerator order matters.
  • Derivative of y terms needs dy/dx.
  • Use radians for trigonometric derivatives.
